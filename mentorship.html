Course selection advice from seniors:

Connecting with senior students can be an invaluable resource when navigating your academic choices. They've already experienced the courses, professors, and academic challenges within your chosen field, giving them unique insights to share. By reaching out to seniors, you can gain personalized recommendations on courses that not only fulfill your degree requirements but also align with your specific interests, major, and minor. They can offer advice on which professors' teaching styles might suit your learning preferences, which courses are particularly challenging or rewarding, and even which electives might be hidden gems. Leveraging their firsthand experience can help you create a more fulfilling and strategically beneficial academic plan.

Networking w seniors & alumni:

Building these professional relationships offers a unique opportunity to gain insider knowledge about your chosen major, explore potential career paths, and receive personalized advice from those who have already walked the path you're considering. Seniors can offer insights into challenging courses, recommend professors, and share their experiences navigating academic life. Alumni, on the other hand, can provide real-world perspectives on career options, industry trends, and the skills needed to succeed after graduation.
